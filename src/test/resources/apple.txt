#* '''1913''', John Weathers, ''Commercial Gardening'', page 38:

#*: This allows a weak plant to benefit from the strong roots of another, or a vigorous tree (such as an '''apple''') to be kept small by growing on 'dwarfing rootstock'.
#* {{quote-book|year=2009|author=Sid Gardner|title=The Faults of the Owens Valley|isbn=9781440177927|page=34|pageurl=http://books.google.com/books?id=gcbQzBOnc0YC&pg=PA34&dq=%22watered+the+apples%22|text=Used to be apple orchards, used to be the river and irrigation ditches that watered the '''apples''', used to be mining towns.}}
